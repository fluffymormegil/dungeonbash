Söťereken 101: General Character

Söťereken is an agglutinative (but not polysynthetic) language with a fairly regular grammar.

Phonology

Söťereken has a moderately sized phoneme inventory, shown in the tables below using the International Phonetic Alphabet.

Notes:

  • The "palatal" series actualy contains both palatal and palato-alveolar sounds.
  • The velar nasal occurs only as an allophone of /n/ before labial consonants.
  • The laterals are not velarized in syllable-final positions
  • Depending on accent, /r/ may be realised as [ɹ], [r], or [ɾ]; it is listed in the approximant row for the sake of conciseness

This substitution is, however, discouraged.

Grammar

Söťereken grammar is agglutinative; case, tense, aspect, etc. are marked by affixes.

Articles

The definite article in Söťereken is <haty>; it is never inflected.

Basic noun declensions

Söťereken nouns are declined on an absolutive-ergative scheme, rather than the nominative-accusative scheme typical of Indo-European languages. The absolutive case (direct object of a verb, or subject of an intransitive verb) is the least-marked form, with no associated affix.

Case markers precede number markers.

Söťereken noun case markers
Case Affix Notes
Absolutive (null) Direct object; intransitive subject
Ergative -Et Transitive subject
Genitive -En Association, origin, possesssion
Vocative -Ep Calling to someone
Dative -at Towards something/someone/somewhere

The E in the affixes above denotes a mid vowel which assimilates to the frontness and roundness of the preceding vowel ([a] is treated as front unrounded).
